Privacy Policy

How Sovereign OS handles your data—which is to say, it doesn't.

The Short Version

We don't collect your data. We can't see your data. We have no servers, no accounts, no analytics, no telemetry, and no way to access or surveil you.

All data is stored locally on your device or synchronized peer-to-peer if you choose to enable it. Encryption is end-to-end. We have no backdoors.

Who We Are

Sovereign OS is a distributed, open-source software project maintained by volunteers. There is no central company, service operator, or data controller. The software is provided as-is under the AGPL-3.0 license.

What This Policy Covers

This policy applies to:

  • Sovereign OS software (running on your device);
  • This website (sovereignos.org);
  • GitHub repositories and discussions;
  • Skill registry and community extensions;
  • Optional cloud backup and sync features (if enabled by you).

Data Collection by Context

Through the Sovereign OS Software

The software collects zero data about your activity. It does not:

  • Send usage statistics or telemetry;
  • Report errors back to any server;
  • Log your queries, documents, or communications;
  • Identify you or track your identity;
  • Contact external services without your explicit request.

All processing happens on your device. No data leaves unless you explicitly sync it (peer-to-peer) or use a skill with network access.

We do not use Google Analytics, Mixpanel, Segment, or any third-party analytics platforms.

Through GitHub

If you open an issue, discussion, or pull request on our GitHub repositories, your activity is governed by GitHub's privacy policy. We have no control over GitHub's data practices.

Through Skill Registry

Community skills (extensions) may have their own privacy policies. When you install a skill, review its permissions and data handling practices. We are not responsible for third-party skills.

Through TDL (Threat Detection Layer)

If you share documents with the TDL for security analysis, you are sending encrypted data to a specified endpoint (if configured). This is your choice and subject to that endpoint's privacy policy.

Data the Software Creates on Your Device

Sovereign OS creates and stores data locally when you use it. This data is entirely under your control:

Documents & Threads
Location: ~/.sovereign/db/

Your notes, documents, threads, and relationships. Stored in SurrealDB, encrypted at rest if configured.

Version History
Location: ~/.sovereign/db/

Historical versions of documents, including timestamps and edit chains. Used for recovery and audit.

AI Profile & Context
Location: ~/.sovereign/profile/

User profile (name, preferences), conversation history with the AI, and learned context. All local.

Session Logs
Location: ~/.sovereign/logs/

Timestamped log of actions, tool calls, and AI responses. Used for debugging and user transparency.

Audit Log
Location: ~/.sovereign/db/

Immutable record of sensitive actions (document access, permission changes, encryption changes). Cannot be deleted.

Encryption Keys
Location: ~/.sovereign/crypto/

Master key, derived keys, key agreement secrets. Secured with OS-level file permissions and optionally a password.

AI Models
Location: ~/.sovereign/models/

Downloaded model weights (Qwen, Whisper, etc.). Several gigabytes. Stored locally, never sent to us.

Voice Data

If you enable voice input (speech-to-text):

  • Recording: Audio is captured and processed locally by Faster Whisper.
  • Transcription: Audio is converted to text on your device. Audio is not sent to any server.
  • Retention: Audio files are not kept after transcription (unless you manually save them).
  • Cloud option: You may choose to use cloud-based speech-to-text (e.g., cloud API). If so, that audio is sent to the cloud provider. This is entirely your choice and responsibility.

Data Sharing

With Us

We do not receive or store any of your data. We have no ability to access your documents, conversations, or profile.

P2P Sync (Optional)

If you enable peer-to-peer (P2P) sync via libp2p, your data is synchronized directly with other Sovereign OS instances you control or trust. This uses encryption to protect data in transit. We do not relay or intercept this data.

Guardian Recovery

If you configure a guardian for recovery, encrypted recovery data is sent to a guardian endpoint (your choice). The guardian cannot decrypt your data without your consent. See Social Recovery for details.

Cloud Backup (Optional)

If you enable cloud backup, encrypted data is uploaded to your chosen cloud provider (e.g., Backblaze, AWS). The cloud provider cannot read your data due to end-to-end encryption. This is your choice.

Skills with Network Access

Some skills (extensions) may request network access. If you grant permission, those skills can connect to external services. Review skill permissions before installing. We are not responsible for skill developer practices.

Third-Party AI Models

Sovereign OS uses AI models for chat, intent classification, and voice processing:

  • Default (Local): Qwen, Whisper, and other models run locally. No data is sent to any server.
  • Optional (Cloud): You can configure cloud-based AI (OpenAI, Anthropic, etc.). If enabled, your queries are sent to that provider. You are responsible for reviewing their privacy policies.

We do not use or store any of your chat data for model training or improvement.

Children's Privacy

Sovereign OS is not designed for children under 13. If a child uses Sovereign OS, a parent or guardian is responsible for their privacy and data protection under applicable laws (e.g., COPPA in the US).

International Data Transfers

All data is stored locally on your device. If you enable P2P sync or cloud backup, data may be transferred to other jurisdictions depending on where peers or cloud services are located. You are responsible for compliance with local data residency requirements (e.g., GDPR, CCPA).
